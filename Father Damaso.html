Character Analysis
Father Damaso was ordained as a Franciscan priest in the Philippines in 1858 and served for three years in a small town. As he left, the indigenous paid tribute to him as a farewell, yet he later found that the natives gave an even greater homage to his replacement, despite spending a shorter time there.
Personality and Characteristics
Padre Damaso was a brash and conceited man. He acted superior to others because of the power bestowed upon him as a monk and was opposed to locals acquiring education overseas. This arrogance was accompanied by roughness, with the guy speaking harsh and even insulting the indigenous of the Philippines at the party of Capitan Tiago, a native himself. Despite this, he had a soft spot for his daughter, breaking up her marriage to Ibarra in order to provide a better life for her.
