Character Analysis
In the story, Crisóstomo Ibarra's father is referenced posthumously. He enrages Father Dámaso, who accuses him of sedition and heresy for criticizing the corrupt practices of the Spanish friars. He dies in jail before his name is cleared. His bones are interred in the Catholic cemetery in San Diego, but Father Dámaso employs a gravedigger to disinter his body so that he can be buried in the Chinese cemetery due to his heretic status.
